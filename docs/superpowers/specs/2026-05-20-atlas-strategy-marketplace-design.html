Atlas v2 · Design Specification

Strategy Marketplace

A self-running marketplace of trading strategies — generated broadly, surfaced honestly.
Date 20 May 2026 Worktree atlas-os-consolidation Branch feat/atlas-consolidation Status Design — pending review Builds on Decision Engine (Waves 1-3), State Engine

01What this is

A self-running marketplace of trading strategies. A generator produces a large population of strategies — hundreds to thousands. Every strategy runs on a paper book from Day Zero. The marketplace surfaces to the fund manager the few that have earned trust: consistent live performance across regimes, plus a clean, inspectable, decision-by-decision audit trail. Strategies that decay are retired; the generator replenishes. No real-money execution in v1 — paper only.

02Why

The fund manager is starting to trust Atlas. He will deploy a strategy only after he can interrogate its consistency across regimes, its logic, and the audit trail behind every decision — not because a leaderboard ranks it #1. The marketplace runs many strategies cheaply and honestly, and surfaces the ones an experienced manager can build conviction in.

03The core distinction

The discipline that keeps this honest. These two layers are not in tension — they are the backend and the frontend of one system.

Generation is broad

Thousands of strategies generated and running on paper. Cheap — the compute is not the constraint. Vectorized over the existing data.

Surfacing is disciplined

What reaches the fund manager is ranked by live forward performance and consistency across regimes — never by backtest rank. A backtest is a starting credential, not a selector.

The rule
A backtest selects nothing. Thousands run; the few that surface as trusted earned it on live, forward, out-of-sample paper results. "Best of thousands" over a short window is luck — so the surfacing filter is breadth-aware: it scales the required live track record to the size of the population and demands consistency across more than one regime.

05Scope — v1

In

  • Generator — samples the Policy-field space (entry states, RS/rank thresholds, sizing caps, exits, universe, cadence). Each strategy is a Policy row. Origin system or manual.
  • Generic vectorized strategy engine — runs any Policy through the 6-step flow, batched across the whole population.
  • Paper-trading from Day Zero — every strategy holds a paper book.
  • Day-Zero credential — a regime-segmented backtest, labelled "not yet live-proven".
  • Live surfacing — marketplace page ranked by live performance + regime consistency.
  • Retirement — decaying strategies retire; generator replenishes.
  • Audit trail — decision-by-decision log. Core scope.

Out of v1

  • Real-money / broker execution. Atlas proposes; v1 stays paper.
  • Fund-manager-authored strategies via a UI builder. v1 manual strategies are authored as config/seed; a UI builder is a later phase.

06The engine — the core build

atlas/trading/lab.py today runs only V5-RP-TREND with four flag modes; it does not consume a parameterized strategy. v1's central engineering is a generic strategy engine that takes any Policy, runs the 6-step decision flow under it, is vectorized (evaluates the whole population over the OHLCV/state panel in batch), and emits — per strategy, per cadence — the trades and the audit-trail rows. Everything else (generator, surfacing page, retirement) is lighter and sits around this engine.

09Data model

  • A strategy is a Policy — atlas_portfolio_policy (migration 092) plus new columns: name, origin (system/manual), lifecycle_status (candidate / surfaced / retired), generated_at.
  • Each strategy owns a paper portfolio — built on the Decision Engine Wave 3 portfolio model + the Wave 4 paper book.
  • Decision audit log — every BUY/TRIM/EXIT per strategy with the triggering signal. Reuse where possible: atlas_strategy_recommendations_daily and atlas_state_action_log already carry per-decision rows. A dedicated atlas_strategy_decision_log is the likely home (resolved in the plan).
  • Performance + ranking — reuse atlas_strategy_leaderboard (incl. regime_breakdown JSONB) and atlas_strategy_validation (per-year backtest).

10Day Zero vs ongoing

Day Zero

The generator seeds the population; each strategy is backtested over ~10y of history, regime-segmented. The page ranks by this backtest credential — explicitly labelled "backtested, not yet live-proven". V5-RP-TREND, already proven, seeds as a manual strategy.

Ongoing

Every strategy paper-trades forward. Live performance accumulates and the ranking shifts from backtest credential to live track record. Both are always shown; live is flagged as the one that counts. Retirement and replenishment run on the live record.

11Success criteria

  1. The generator seeds and maintains a population of hundreds+ strategies, all paper-trading.
  2. The marketplace page surfaces strategies ranked by live performance, filtered by regime consistency; the fund manager sees the trusted few.
  3. Any strategy can be opened and every paper decision traced to its signal.
  4. Backtest vs live-paper is unambiguous everywhere.
  5. Decaying strategies retire automatically; the generator replenishes.

12Open questions

Generator sampling. Pure random over Policy-space, grid, or mutate live-winners (genetic). v1 can start with random + grid; mutation later.
Vectorized engine boundaries. How much of the 6-step flow vectorizes cleanly vs needs a per-strategy loop. An implementation-plan decision.
Audit-log storage. Dedicated atlas_strategy_decision_log vs extending atlas_strategy_recommendations_daily. An implementation-plan decision.
