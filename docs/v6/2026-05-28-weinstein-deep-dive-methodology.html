Weinstein Deep-Dive — Entry-Point Methodology

Status: design draft → Stream A2 dispatch  |  Authored: 2026-05-28 (after Stream A1 reported IC fails 0.05 floor)  |  Supersedes: the simple "stage tagging" approach in Stream A plan

1. Why the first pass failed

Stream A1 tested whether the four-stage Weinstein classifier (Stage 1 / 2 / 3 / 4) predicts forward 6-month returns. Pooled Pearson IC across all 12 (cap × lookback) combinations failed the 0.05 floor. Best was Large 30W at −0.043.

The failure is a methodology gap, not a Weinstein gap. We treated stage tag as the predictor. But Weinstein never claimed the steady-state stage tag is the signal — he claimed the Stage 1 → Stage 2 transition, with confirmation, is the signal. We measured the wrong thing.

Read this as good news. Stage 2 average excess vs Nifty-500 was monotone-positive across all cap-tiers (Large +5.9%, Mid +6.9%, Small +10.2% at 30W). The alpha IS in Stage 2. We just need a sharper detector for when a stock enters Stage 2, not a tag that says "currently in Stage 2."

2. What Weinstein actually said

Stan Weinstein, Secrets for Profiting in Bull and Bear Markets (1988), defines the buy point with these rules (paraphrased):

  1. Stock has been in a Stage 1 base. Price has chopped sideways above and below a roughly flat 30-week MA for an extended period (multiple months minimum). No fresh lows.
  2. Stock breaks out above the top of the trading range. The base resistance — typically defined as the highest weekly close over the last 10-30 weeks — is taken out on a closing basis.
  3. The 30W MA is flat or rising at the time of breakout. Not still falling.
  4. Volume confirms. Breakout week volume materially exceeds prior-base average volume (typically 1.5× to 2×).
  5. Relative strength is improving. The stock is outperforming the broader market into the breakout, not lagging.
  6. Industry group is also basing or breaking out. Leaders in leading groups, not lone-wolf breakouts.

Most pseudo-Weinstein implementations capture rule 3 alone ("price > 30W MA and MA rising"). That is necessary but not sufficient. The signal is the conjunction of all six. Each filter, applied alone, gives weak IC. Applied together, the filtered events are sparse but high-conviction.

3. The right unit of measurement: the transition event

Reframe the research question:

Wrong questionRight question
"Across all dates a stock is tagged Stage 2, what is the average forward 6m return?" "On the specific date a stock enters Stage 2 with confirmation, what is the forward 6m return?"
Tens of thousands of observations per cap-tier Hundreds of events per cap-tier per year, max
Smears alpha across mid-trend dates Isolates the entry point, where alpha is concentrated

An "event" is a date D for instrument i when the transition fires. Forward return is measured from close(D) to close(D + horizon), vs benchmark. IC is computed across the event population (small N, high signal-to-noise — opposite of the dense steady-state population).

4. Transition detector — base rule + confluence layers

4.1 Base rule (Stage 1 → Stage 2 crossover)

A "Weinstein breakout" event fires on date D for instrument i when ALL hold:

  (a) close(D)     >  MA_chosen(D)
  (b) close(D-1)   <= MA_chosen(D-1)              # crossover this week
  (c) slope(MA, 4-week lookback)(D)  >= 0          # MA flat or rising
  (d) Stage_1_persistence(D, 4 weeks back) >= 0.8  # was in Stage 1 (or unclassified)
                                                   # for >= 80% of prior 4 weeks
                                                   # → kills V-bottom false signals

Where MA_chosen ∈ {10W, 20W, 30W} per cap_tier.

This already drops the event count by 50-80% vs the steady-state Stage 2 population. The Stage_1_persistence filter (rule d) is the critical addition versus a naive crossover detector — it forces the prior structure to be a base, not a falling knife caught mid-air.

4.2 Confluence layers (additive, test each separately)

For each base event, also test whether the following layered filters lift IC. Each filter is binary (pass/fail); we measure IC of base events that pass each filter individually and in combination.

LayerRuleWhat it filters out
L1 — Volume volume(D, 5-day MA) ≥ 1.5 × volume(D, 60-day MA) Quiet-day breakouts (no buyer follow-through)
L2 — Prior-high clearance close(D) > max(close, last 13 weeks excluding D) Failed breakouts that get sold immediately (no fresh demand)
L3 — RS improving rs_rank_3m(D) > rs_rank_3m(D - 4 weeks) Breakouts in laggards (only buy leaders)
L4 — Base width Stage 1 persistence prior 12 weeks ≥ 0.7 Short bases (more reliable signals come from longer accumulations)
L5 — Sector confirmation Linked sector RS rank improving over prior 4 weeks Lone-wolf breakouts where the sector context is weak
L6 — Liquidity floor avg daily traded value over prior 20d ≥ ₹5 Cr (Large) / ₹2 Cr (Mid) / ₹50 L (Small) Illiquid breakouts that we can't actually act on

4.3 The 50W rule was the wrong proxy

The user flagged that "price > 50W high" will eliminate too many candidates. Correct. The right operationalization of Weinstein's "breaks above base resistance" is L2 — clears prior 13W close-high, not 50W high. 13W (~3 months) matches the typical Stage 1 base length without requiring the stock to set an all-time high. Most actionable breakouts are continuation moves through prior 3-month resistance, not new-all-time-high moves.

4.4 Anti-Weinstein (Stage 3→4) — symmetric for SELL signals

The same framework applies in reverse for SELL signals. A Stage 3→4 transition event fires when:

  (a') close(D)     <  MA_chosen(D)
  (b') close(D-1)   >= MA_chosen(D-1)               # downside crossover
  (c') slope(MA, 4-week)(D) <= 0                    # MA flat or falling
  (d') Stage_3_persistence(D, 4 weeks back) >= 0.8  # was topping, not just one bad week

With anti-confluences:
  L1' — volume EXPANSION on the breakdown (institutional distribution)
  L2' — clears prior 13W close-LOW (genuine breakdown, not noise)
  L3' — RS DEGRADING over prior 4 weeks
  L4' — Stage 3 width (topping period) ≥ 12 weeks

5. Research plan — Stream A2

Discovery exercise applied to Weinstein, modeled on the existing cell-discovery framework in CONTEXT.md §"24-framework discovery model" (lines 540-580).

Step 1 — Cap-tier point-in-time fix

Static atlas_universe_stocks.tier introduces survivor bias. Backfill atlas_scorecard_daily.cap_tier to 2018 from trailing-60d median traded value before kicking off Step 2. Parallel workstream.

Step 2 — Build the event detector

SQL view atlas.v_weinstein_events_base that flags every base-rule transition event (Stage 1→2 up, Stage 3→4 down) per instrument per date per cap-tier per MA-lookback.

Step 3 — Apply confluence layers

For each base event, compute boolean flags for L1-L6. Persist in atlas.weinstein_event_features.

Step 4 — IC per (cap_tier × lookback × confluence-set)

Compute forward 6m excess return per event. Group by (cap_tier × lookback × confluence-subset). Compute IC (Spearman, since events are sparse and outlier-sensitive). Output: ranked table of rule combinations.

Confluence subsets to test (16 combinations per cap-tier × lookback, not 64 — restrict to monotone-additive layering):

  • Base alone
  • Base + each of L1-L6 individually (6 cases)
  • Base + L1+L2 (volume + prior-high) (1 case)
  • Base + L1+L3 (volume + RS) (1 case)
  • Base + L2+L3 (prior-high + RS) (1 case)
  • Base + L1+L2+L3 (volume + prior-high + RS — Weinstein "full"); (1 case)
  • Base + L4 (base width) (1 case)
  • Base + L1+L2+L3+L4 (1 case)
  • Base + L1+L2+L3+L4+L6 (full minus L5 — leave sector confluence for follow-up since it requires sector-level RS data not yet wired) (1 case)

Step 5 — Event-count floor

A rule combination wins only if:

  • IC ≥ 0.05 at 6m (CONTEXT.md floor)
  • Event count ≥ 50 per cap_tier per year (i.e. ~400+ over the 8-year window) so the signal is actually actionable on a portfolio-rebalancing cadence
  • Hit rate (% of events with forward return > 0) ≥ 60%

Step 6 — Walk-forward

Once a rule combination wins on in-sample IC, validate via 3-year-train / 1-year-test rolling windows from 2018-2026. Out-of-sample IC must remain above floor.

Step 7 — Lock the winners

Per cap_tier, lock the best (MA-lookback × confluence-set) combination into atlas_thresholds. The verdict composer (Stream C) will read these to fire WAIT vs BUY decisions.

6. What this changes downstream

The verdict composer's Weinstein veto logic in spec §4 needs to be updated. Today's plan says:

  if cell_state == POSITIVE and weinstein_stage == 4:
      verdict = WAIT

After this research, the logic becomes richer:

  weinstein_signal_today = compute_weinstein_event(instrument, today, locked_rule_set)
  # Returns one of: BREAKOUT_FIRED, BREAKDOWN_FIRED, NONE, IN_STAGE_2, IN_STAGE_4

  if cell_state == POSITIVE:
      if weinstein_signal == BREAKDOWN_FIRED:        verdict = WAIT, reason = "Weinstein breakdown today"
      elif weinstein_signal == IN_STAGE_4:           verdict = WAIT, reason = "Stage 4 — wait for Stage 2 confirmation"
      elif weinstein_signal == BREAKOUT_FIRED:       verdict = BUY,  reason = "Cell POS + Weinstein breakout confirmed"
      else:                                          verdict = BUY  # cell alone is enough

The methodology page (Stream E) gets a much richer Weinstein section — not just "Stage 2 = uptrend" but "here are the 6 rules, here are the lookbacks per cap-tier, here is the IC of the locked rule set, here are the worked breakout examples."

7. Honesty

This research can fail. Plausible failure modes:

  • No confluence combination clears the 0.05 IC floor → Weinstein is genuinely under-powered for Indian equities given current data. Fall back to cell-only verdicts; document.
  • Event counts too low when confluence is tight → signal is real but not actionable at the portfolio cadence; document and let cell math drive.
  • Walk-forward IC degrades severely → in-sample fit was a bull-market artifact; document and pick a more conservative rule.

If any of these happen, the right next step is to document the limitation and let the cell-IC composite carry the load. Weinstein becomes a context chip ("Stage 2 confirmed" / "Stage 4 — caution"), not a hard veto on the verdict.
